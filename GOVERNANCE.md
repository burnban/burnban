# Project governance

Burnban is an MIT-licensed maintainer-led project. The current lead maintainer
is Oday Brahem (`@OdayBrahem`).

## Decisions

Routine changes are decided through issue and pull-request discussion. The lead
maintainer makes the final call when consensus is not clear, with priority given
to user safety, local control, compatibility, maintainability, and the explicit
open-source/paid-product boundary.

Material changes should be proposed publicly before implementation when
practical. These include license changes, telemetry, default network exposure,
credential handling, destructive data behavior, governance changes, and removal
or paywalling of an existing open-source feature.

Security fixes may be developed privately under the process in
[SECURITY.md](SECURITY.md) and documented when released.

## Maintainer responsibilities

Maintainers review contributions, enforce community standards, operate releases,
triage vulnerabilities, keep dependency and pricing data current, and disclose
conflicts of interest. A maintainer must not approve their own change to release
permissions, artifact signing, or security policy without another qualified
review when such a reviewer is available.

## Becoming a maintainer

Sustained contributors may be invited based on technical judgment, respectful
collaboration, reliability, and care for security/privacy boundaries. Access is
granted incrementally and can be removed for inactivity, compromised accounts,
or violations of project policy.

## Commercial relationship

The repository is MIT licensed and accepts contributions on that basis. Burnban
Teams and other separately distributed products may reuse MIT code, just as any
other person or company may. Commercial priorities do not override the license
or secretly change behavior in the open-source binary.

Changes to this governance document use the normal pull-request process and
should remain open for community comment before merging unless they address an
urgent security or legal issue.
